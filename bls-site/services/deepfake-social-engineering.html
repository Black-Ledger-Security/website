Novel Capability

Deepfake AI Social Engineering

Next-generation social engineering using voice cloning and video deepfake technology to test whether your organization can detect AI-generated impersonation attacks.

The Emerging Threat

Deepfake-enabled vishing and video calls represent a rapidly emerging threat that most organizations have no defenses against. Voice cloning technology has become accessible, affordable, and convincing enough to impersonate executives, vendors, and trusted contacts over the phone.

Our engagements demonstrate the real-world risk and help you build detection and verification procedures before an actual adversary exploits this capability against you.

What We Test

We use AI-generated voice cloning to conduct social engineering calls that impersonate known individuals within or connected to your organization. Testing evaluates whether employees can distinguish AI-generated voices from real ones, whether existing verification procedures are followed, and whether current security awareness training addresses this threat vector.

Engagement Structure

Engagements begin with target selection and voice sample collection from publicly available sources. We develop attack scenarios that mirror realistic threat actor operations: executive impersonation for financial fraud, vendor impersonation for credential harvesting, and internal impersonation for physical access or information disclosure.

What You Receive

Detailed documentation of every attack attempt including audio samples, employee responses, and outcomes. Assessment of your organization's current detection capability against AI-generated impersonation. Specific recommendations for verification procedures, awareness training, and technical controls to defend against deepfake-enabled social engineering.
