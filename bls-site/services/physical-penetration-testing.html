Covert Operations

Physical Penetration Testing

Full-scope covert intrusion testing against your physical security controls. We simulate the complete attack chain from initial reconnaissance through physical compromise.

What We Test

Our operators conduct real-world breach attempts using the same techniques employed by sophisticated threat actors: badge cloning, lock bypass, tailgating, after-hours entry, and objective-based operations inside your facilities.

Every engagement is scoped around specific objectives. Whether the goal is accessing a server room, exfiltrating sensitive documents, or planting a network implant, we simulate the complete attack chain from initial reconnaissance through physical compromise.

How It Works

Physical penetration tests begin with detailed reconnaissance of your facility, access control systems, and personnel patterns. We identify entry vectors, develop pretexts, and prepare the tools and documentation needed for the operation.

During execution, our operators attempt to gain unauthorized access to your facility and reach predefined objectives. Every action is documented with timestamped notes and photographic evidence.

What You Receive

You receive a detailed report documenting every vulnerability exploited, with photographic evidence and prioritized remediation guidance. The report covers the full attack narrative from planning through execution, identifies systemic weaknesses in your physical security program, and provides specific recommendations for both quick fixes and long-term improvements.

Common Objectives

Server room access and data exfiltration. Network implant deployment. Sensitive document retrieval. Executive floor access. Badge cloning and credential harvesting. After-hours facility breach. Loading dock and service entrance exploitation.
