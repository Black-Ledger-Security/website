About Black Ledger Security

Founded by a Marine Corps veteran and career penetration tester with over 15 years of experience across cybersecurity, intelligence, and special operations.

The Mission

Most security programs are built around technology. Firewalls. EDR. SIEM. Vulnerability scanners. These tools matter. But they are designed to defend systems, not people.

They do not stop the adversary who studies your organization, profiles your personnel, identifies the most susceptible targets, and engineers opportunistic access through disciplined psychological tradecraft.

Black Ledger Security exists to test the threats that operate outside your technical perimeter.

We exploit real weaknesses where they exist, but we do not stop there. We design targeted, threat-informed simulations that demonstrate how compromise would actually unfold inside your organization.

Our engagements are built to create measurable improvement and memorable impact across both technical teams and employees. Clients leave not only with actionable remediation strategies, but with a clear, lasting understanding of how real adversaries operate.

Every engagement is designed to leave your organization more resilient than when it began.

The Founder

Black Ledger Security was founded by a career offensive security professional who spent over a decade in the United States Marine Corps before transitioning to the private sector. Military assignments included Marine Corps Forces Special Operations Command (MARSOC), where he served on a Marine Special Operations Team in direct support of special operations and intelligence agency missions across multiple theaters and joint task forces, with deployments to the CENTCOM and INDOPACOM areas of responsibility.

Since transitioning to private sector cybersecurity in 2021, he has led social engineering teams, conducted solo physical penetration tests against secured corporate and government facilities, and delivered hundreds of offensive security assessments for clients across financial services, healthcare, manufacturing, defense, and critical infrastructure. His current focus is on how large language models, deepfake technology, and agentic AI systems are reshaping the offensive security landscape.

He is the author of Dark Dossier, a weekly threat intelligence publication covering AI security, social engineering tradecraft, and emerging attack techniques.

Why Black Ledger

We are not a generalist IT security firm. We do not sell antivirus, manage your firewall, or monitor your SIEM. We are offensive specialists. Every engagement is designed to answer one question: what would happen if a motivated adversary targeted your people, your facilities, or your AI systems?

Our background in special operations and intelligence gives us a perspective that most security consultants do not have. We think like adversaries because we trained as operators. We plan methodically, execute precisely, and report honestly.

Black Ledger Security is a Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (SDVOSB) based in Charleston, South Carolina.
